The Threat Landscape

UPI Scams Are Evolving
Faster Than Most People Realize

Modern scammers don't hack your bank account directly—they hack your trust and behavior. Here are the 4 most common traps this guide helps you defeat.

Fake Payment Screenshots

Scammers use specialized spoof apps to generate realistic success screens, fooling sellers into handing over goods without receiving the cash.

QR Code Traps

"Scan to receive money." Scammers send a QR code that triggers a *debit* transaction instead of credit, stealing your balance in seconds.

Remote Access Fraud

Under the guise of bank support, callers guide targets to install screen-sharing apps like AnyDesk, capturing passwords and OTPs live.

Social Engineering

Fake fear elements like threat of power bills cutting off, tax collection, or custom duties. They manipulate your panic state to initiate payments.

Chapter 1: The Anatomy of a Scam

1.1 The Golden Rule: UPI PIN is Only for Paying

Page 12 — UPI Safety Handbook

Understanding the core mechanism of UPI is your first and most absolute layer of protection. There is one fundamental technical truth that scammers will always try to hide from you:

🛡️ Core Security rule

Entering your UPI PIN always DEBITS money from your account. You NEVER need to enter your PIN to receive money.

If a buyer claims they are sending you money, and asks you to enter your PIN or scans a "received QR code," the transaction is a debit setup. Real credit transactions happen instantly without any actions on the receiver's end.

1.2 Anatomy of the QR Code Trap

Page 14 — UPI Safety Handbook

The QR code trap starts with an listing on classifieds websites (OLX, Facebook Marketplace). A scammer contact you acting eager to purchase your item.

They claim they cannot visit you in person, and want to send advance payment. They send you an image of a QR code via WhatsApp with text reading:

[WhatsApp Message received]
"Sir, I have scanned and sent money. Please scan this receipt code to confirm payment in your bank account."

When you scan this code with your UPI app, a prompt appears showing: Paying: sandeep_store@okaxis.

Instead of reading this carefully, people are pressured by the scammer on the phone, who says: "Click OK, click PAY, and type your PIN so the transaction registers."
